# Pipedrive: Real Estate CRM Guide

Source: https://www.pipedrive.com/en/blog/real-estate-crm
Fetched: March 2026

## Pipeline types agents use (multiple pipelines)
- Properties pipeline — track listings and details
- Investors pipeline — manage investor relationships
- Buyers pipeline — track preferences, timelines, budgets
- Sellers pipeline — monitor interactions and property valuations

## Buyer pipeline stages
1. Lead capture (web forms, inquiries, open houses)
2. Qualification (property preferences, budget, timeline)
3. Property matching & notifications
4. Showing / open house attendance
5. Negotiation and offer management
6. Post-sale follow-up and gifting

## Seller pipeline stages
1. Initial consultation and listing setup
2. Property information organization (photos, details, docs)
3. Buyer inquiry management
4. Offer tracking and negotiation
5. Transaction documentation and eSign
6. Move-in day follow-up

## Key fields agents capture
- Property details and photographs
- Client communication histories (calls, emails, messages)
- Budget and timeline
- Neighborhood preferences
- Proximity preferences (schools, hospitals)
- Contract and lease documentation
- Payment tracking (deadlines, overdue flags)
- Lead source / referral origin
- Last contact date

## Daily agent activities (what they actually use)
- Taking audio notes between showings
- Reviewing conversation history before client meetings
- Setting and receiving automated follow-up reminders
- Tracking deal progress and transaction status
- Managing documents with eSignature
- Receiving alerts when listings match buyer criteria
- Mobile access for on-site updates
- Checking email open/click rates to gauge buyer interest

## Automation patterns agents use
- Automated drip email campaigns for lead nurturing
- Reminders triggered after open house attendance
- Follow-up sequences when deal reaches a new stage
- Alerts when expected listings enter the market
- Payment deadline reminders
